PROP points & rebuttals 
A: Cameras do little to reduce crime.
R: A study from New York University, for one, found that
   cameras installed in two privately owned apartment complexes
   in New York City were an ineffective crime deterrent.
E: The Electronic Frontier Foundation. Article #1

A: San Francisco's ordinance posts threats to consumer privacy.
R: Although the legislation needs the police to get permission
   from the camera owners before viewing live footage, the police
   have been able to obtain Ring recordings directly from Amazon.
E: Matt Guariglia, a policy analyst at the foundation.
   Article #1

A: The effectiveness of security camera systems are often
   compromised by lack of investment.
R: Cameras can fall into disrepair without dedicated funding for
   maintenance, and school staff don't usually receive
   meaningful training on how to use the systems.
E: Kenneth Trump, a school security expert and president of
   National School Safety and Security Services. Article #2

A: Cameras are easily abused.
R: They can be used for voyeurism, act of stalking, and a law
   enforcement blackmailing another officer.
E: Amanda Li's "Top 8 Pros and Cons of Public Surveillance
   Cameras" Article #4
OPP points & rebuttals 
A: Public security cameras bring convenience for everyday life.
R: Not only do these devices provide evidence of crimes, but
   also they can be used to prevent lawbreakers from running a
   red light in traffic. Other than reducing death rates from
   car accidents, security cameras can provide quicker routes.
E: Amanda Li's "Top 8 Pros and Cons of Public Surveillance
   Cameras" Article #4
